The Wayward Bus

Liam Nicol

Information about my Mapbox studio basemap.

Here is a look into the spec sheet and inspiration for this aesthetic. I drew inspiration from cartoonish and pastel album art. Not only do these albums convey a visual aesthetic, but the music itself reinforces the aesthetic as well. So, I wanted to create a basemap that doesn't just make me think of the visual art, but makes me think of the sounds of the album as well.

In the map, roads are deep purple, land is light green with a stitch pattern, parks are yellow, and hillshade is a lighter purple. The larger areas of land are labeled with a video game-like font.

Let's see what a few different places look like with the basemap.

As you can see, zooming in makes the land much lighter in comparison to the smaller scales. Several layers not related to the components were added, such as pink areas representing hospitals, brown to show universities, and navy blue for airports.

I really wanted to convey the aesthetic of a whimsical, cartoonish pastel drawing. So, I used light colors to represent landscape attributes, with darker colors mostly being limited to the built landscape. You may also notice a stitch-like pattern on some aspects of the landscape. This was chosen to make the basemap feel crafted and/or painted.

I used Merriweather Bold font to represent larger scale labels because I thought it most looked like the text on the Wayward Bus album cover. For smaller scale labels, I used Press Start font to reinforce the imaginary feel to the aesthetic. Intially, I had these swapped, but found the Press Start font too overbearing for large scale labels.
